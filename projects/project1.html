Project 1

Course Code and Name: MSJ 11342: Public Relations Research
Project Name: “Evaluating the Sustenance Quality of ULAB Campus: From the Perspective of ULAB Public Relations”
Date of Submission: Summer 2022

Project Description: This research aims to evaluate the quality and availability of food services at ULAB from the perspective of its students. It explores how the campus sustenance facilities affect students’ physical and mental well-being, daily performance, and overall satisfaction with campus life. The study will gather feedback from students to identify areas where ULAB can improve in providing nutritious, affordable, and accessible food options. The ultimate goal is to raise awareness about the importance of sustenance in student life and to support ULAB’s commitment to creating a healthier, more supportive academic environment.

Project Justification: Sustenance plays a vital role in maintaining both physical and mental well-being, which directly impacts students’ academic performance and overall productivity. However, many university students experience stress, poor eating habits, and lack of proper nutrition due to inadequate food facilities on campus. This project is justified by the need to evaluate whether the University of Liberal Arts Bangladesh (ULAB) provides sufficient and quality sustenance options for its students. Understanding this issue can help identify gaps and encourage the university to improve its food services, thereby promoting better mental health, satisfaction, and academic outcomes among students.

Learnings and Outcomes: Through this research, we learned that while most ULAB students are generally satisfied with the canteen food, some concerns exist regarding management and service quality. We gained insights into how students perceive campus sustenance and how feedback can influence improvements. The study highlighted the importance of collecting primary data to understand real experiences and needs. As an outcome, our findings can help ULAB authorities make informed decisions to enhance food quality, menu variety, and overall student satisfaction, contributing to better mental and physical well-being on campus.
