Project 11

Course Code and Name: MSJ11401: Development Communication
Project Name: Career Stress on Students Cause Unwanted Repercussions
Date of Project: Fall 2021

Project Description: The campaign “Career Stress on Students Causes Unwanted Repercussions” focuses on raising awareness about the mental, emotional, and social challenges faced by Bangladeshi students aged 22 to 28 due to academic and career-related pressures. Many students in this age group experience anxiety and depression when they are unable to meet the expectations set by their families, friends, and society. The project aims to shed light on how excessive pressure—through questioning, comparison, or criticism—can lead to serious mental health issues such as loss of confidence, social withdrawal, depression, and self-harm tendencies. Using data collected through surveys, the campaign emphasizes the importance of understanding and empathy from families and peers. Communication tools such as awareness posters were used to visually and emotionally connect with the target audience. The posters served as an impactful medium to spread the message that emotional support, not pressure, helps students achieve success and well-being.

Project Justification: This project was initiated to highlight a growing social and psychological issue that often remains overlooked in Bangladeshi culture. In a society where academic excellence and job status define self-worth, students face constant judgment and expectations. These figures underline the urgency of addressing the problem through awareness and dialogue. By visually representing these findings in posters and campaigns, we aimed to initiate behavioral change among families and friends and encourage compassion and mental health awareness in the community.

Learnings and Outcomes: Through this project, we learned that family and peer pressure have a profound impact on students’ mental health and self-esteem. Many families unintentionally cause stress by setting unrealistic expectations, believing it motivates their children, while friends sometimes add to the pressure through comparison and mockery. We realized that open communication, emotional support, and understanding are essential for helping students overcome anxiety and depression related to career decisions. The campaign’s outcome was positive, as it successfully raised awareness about the psychological struggles students face and encouraged families and peers to be more empathetic. It also promoted healthy dialogue, reduced stigma around mental health, and inspired students to seek help and make independent, confident career choices.
