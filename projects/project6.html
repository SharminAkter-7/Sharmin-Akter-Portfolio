Project 6

Course Code and Name: MSJ11344 Internal Communication
Project Name: Final Assignment of Internal Communication
Date of Submission/Project: Fall 2022

Project Description: The project focused on analyzing the internal communication practices of the Student Affairs Office (SAO) at ULAB. It aimed to understand how the office manages communication between employees, mentors, and students to maintain a healthy organizational atmosphere. The study involved conducting interviews with two Peer Mentors from the MSJ department, Fariza Ekram and Fairuz Tahasin Anika, who are actively working with SAO. The interviews explored areas such as diversity and inclusiveness, employee behavior, well-being and satisfaction, internal conflict management, work ethics, organizational goals, and formal/informal communication practices. The goal was to identify effective communication strategies, understand challenges faced by the employees, and propose recommendations to improve organizational efficiency, employee satisfaction, and student engagement.

Project Justification: Effective internal communication is crucial for any organization, especially in educational institutions where employee-student interaction directly affects student satisfaction and success. The Student Affairs Office plays a key role in guiding and supporting students, making it essential to assess how communication flows within the office and between staff and students. The project was justified by the need to improve internal communication, which is essential for the smooth functioning of educational institutions where employee-student interaction significantly impacts student satisfaction and success. Assessing SAO’s communication practices helps identify gaps, enhance coordination among staff, and ensure better delivery of services to students. Additionally, understanding employee satisfaction, inclusivity, diversity, and conflict resolution mechanisms provides valuable insights for creating a supportive and fair work environment. The study also aimed to provide evidence-based recommendations to guide policy improvements and strengthen organizational performance.

Learnings and Outcomes: The project revealed that the Student Affairs Office (SAO) effectively uses various communication channels to support staff, mentors, and students, creating a generally positive work environment. However, areas like workload management, salaries, office infrastructure, and employee training need improvement. The project highlighted the importance of inclusiveness, diversity, and ethical practices in enhancing employee satisfaction and organizational efficiency. Through this work, valuable learning was gained in research, analysis, and understanding effective internal communication within an educational organization.
